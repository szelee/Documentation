CbeMobileServicePurchaseGiftEVouchers Method

For example,.

1) Customer can purchase in denominations £50 x 5, £10 x 20 eVouchers i.e. total 25 eVouchers.

OR.

2) Customer can purchase fixed amount of £75 x 1 voucher i.e 1 eVoucher.

OR.

3) Customer can purchase fixed amount of £75 x 1 voucher and denomination £50 x 2 vouchers i.e. 3 eVouchers.

Remarks

BACKGROUND

eVouchers are secure digital tokens that carry a monetary value and can be used as a method of payment for the purchase of Rail and related products. eVouchers may take the form of:

  • Gift Vouchers
  • Customer Compensation vouchers
  • Gesture of Goodwill (GoGW)
  • Promotional Vouchers

eVouchers are currently being used by VTEC. They can be purchased as a Gift vouchers, issued by Customer Services as compensation vouchers or issued by Marketing as promotional vouchers.

Gift vouchers are purchased by a registered customer and usually gifted to a second individual who may register the voucher on their account or use it as a method of payment without claiming the voucher. Currently eVouchers are not used for Delay / Repay compensation.

PROCESS DESCRIPTION

This API allows the customer to purchase electronic gift vouchers, in the form of eVouchers for rail travel products from the CBE. The eVouchers are in set denominations determined by the TOC or may be for a different fixed amount defined by the customer. It allows collection of eVoucher codes to be specified. This collection should contain only a single eVoucher of type Loyalty

For example:

  • Customer can purchase in denominations £50 x 5, £10 x 20 eVouchers i.e. total 25 eVouchers.
  • Customer can purchase fixed amount of £75 x 1 voucher i.e 1 eVoucher.
  • Customer can purchase fixed amount of £75 x 1 voucher and denomination £50 x 2 vouchers i.e. 3 eVouchers.

A customer can purchase one or more eVoucher in a sales transaction.

For each eVoucher requested by this FEWS the CBE will generate a eVoucher code.

The value in the input is a standard denomination OR a custom value entered by the customer. Where the value in the input is a custom value, the CBE will check that the value does not exceed the maximum allowed for “Gift” type eVouchers.

The CBE will calculate the expiry date, by adding the default period for “Gift” type eVouchers to the current date and return that in the output.

When the eVoucher is created:

  • The purchase date will be the current date
  • The eVoucher Type will be set to “Gift”
  • The eVoucher Value will be from the input request

The output confirms to the customer the details of the eVouchers purchased.

ASSUMPTIONS

  1. It is assumed that a sales transaction will contain one or more eVouchers and no other products.
  2. It is assumed that the eVoucher purchased will be of a multi-use type so that the same eVoucher can be used for multiple transactions provided the voucher has enough remaining balance.

VALIDATION

Parameter Validation:

  1. None

Business Validation:

  1. All mandatory fields are present.
  2. Customer ID must exist as a customer account.
  3. Total value in the eVoucher details must equal total value of eVoucher purchase data item values
  4. Denomination of eVouchers must be valid denominations (See Notes)
  5. Either eVoucher denomination or eVoucher Custom Value must be populated.
  6. Sales point details must be valid.
  7. eVoucher Custom Value must be less than the maximum Custom Value for eVouchers (See Notes)
  8. If EVoucherCollection is present the only the one Loyalty evoucher can be there.
  9. The eVoucher must exist and be associated with the customer and have a status of “Claimed”
  10. The eVoucher must not be expired or Voided
  11. The eVoucher must be redeemable through used retail channel.
